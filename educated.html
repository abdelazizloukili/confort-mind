Mental Health Issues

Depression

Depression is a mood disorder that causes persistent feelings of sadness, emptiness, and a loss of enjoyment in activities you used to find pleasurable. It's more than just feeling down for a few days. People with depression may also experience changes in sleep, appetite, energy levels, and concentration

Read More

Anxiety

This is a feeling of nervousness, worry, or unease that can be constant or come in waves. Physical symptoms of anxiety can include a racing heart, sweating, shortness of breath, and muscle tension. People with anxiety disorders may also experience excessive worry about everyday situations or phobias of specific things.

Read More

Stress

This is a feeling of pressure or strain. While some stress is normal, chronic stress can take a toll on your mental and physical health. Symptoms of stress can include anxiety, headaches, muscle tension, fatigue, and difficulty sleeping.

Read More

Bipolar

This condition is characterized by extreme mood swings, ranging from mania (feeling very energetic, elated, and impulsive) to depression (feeling sad, hopeless, and sluggish). These episodes can last for days, weeks, or even months.

Read More

Insomnia

This is a sleep disorder that makes it difficult to fall asleep or stay asleep. People with insomnia may experience daytime fatigue, irritability, and difficulty concentrating.

Read More

Eating Disorders

These are conditions characterized by unhealthy eating habits and a distorted body image. Common eating disorders include anorexia nervosa (restricting food intake), bulimia nervosa (binge eating and purging), and binge-eating disorder.
